## 1. Purpose of the Event

**AWS First Cloud AI Journey Community Day 2026** was organized to connect students, developers, engineers, and cloud enthusiasts while introducing modern technologies in Cloud Computing, Platform Engineering, DevOps, and Generative AI on AWS.

The event featured multiple technical sessions delivered by industry professionals, providing participants with insights into current technology trends, career opportunities, and real-world applications of AWS services.

Beyond the technical content, the event also created an inspiring environment where participants could interact with the AWS community, exchange ideas, and strengthen their motivation to pursue careers in cloud technologies.

---

## 2. Organizers and Participants

The event was organized by the **AWS First Cloud AI Journey** community with support from technology partners and industry professionals.

The speakers represented different areas of expertise, including:

- Platform Engineering
- Cloud Architecture
- DevOps Engineering
- Software Engineering
- Generative AI
- Cloud Networking and Security

Participants included:

- University students from various institutions.
- Members of the AWS First Cloud AI Journey program.
- Software engineers and cloud practitioners.
- Individuals interested in AWS Cloud, Artificial Intelligence, and DevOps.

---

## 3. Highlights of the Event

The event consisted of several technical sessions covering modern cloud technologies and software engineering practices.

### Building Modern Platform Engineering & Career Pathways

This session introduced Platform Engineering, company culture, internship opportunities, and career development in the cloud industry.

It also explained how Platform Engineering helps development teams build, deploy, and operate cloud applications more efficiently.

### GenAIOps Essential – DevOps for Generative AI Applications

The speakers introduced the concept of GenAIOps and demonstrated how DevOps practices are applied to Generative AI applications on AWS.

Several AWS technologies such as Amazon Bedrock, observability tools, EKS, and AI-related monitoring solutions were also mentioned.

### Shipping Code in the Agentic Era

This presentation discussed how software development is evolving with AI-assisted programming, covering modern development tools, productivity improvements, and practical demonstrations.

### Production-Grade Multimodal GenAI on AWS

The session introduced the architecture of modern AI applications, including Multimodal Search, Nova Embeddings, GraphRAG, Multi-Agent Workflows, and techniques for building reliable and observable GenAI systems.

### From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

The final presentation focused on Amazon CloudFront and explained how it improves application performance, security, reliability, and cost optimization for workloads deployed on AWS.

## 4. What I Learned

Although many of the technical topics were quite advanced and difficult for me to fully understand at that time, the event still provided valuable learning experiences.

After attending the event, I realized that:

- Cloud Computing, DevOps, and Generative AI are becoming increasingly important in the technology industry.
- Modern software engineering involves much more than writing code; deployment, monitoring, automation, and security are equally important.
- There are many exciting career paths in cloud technologies, such as Platform Engineer, Cloud Engineer, DevOps Engineer, and AI Engineer.
- Continuous learning and hands-on practice are essential for keeping up with rapidly evolving technologies.
- Technology communities play a significant role in helping beginners learn, grow, and connect with professionals.

## 5. Most Memorable Experience

The most memorable part of the event was not the technical presentations themselves, but a special moment involving a group of **hearing-impaired students from Nguyen Tat Thanh University**.

Despite the communication challenges they face in daily life, these students confidently came on stage to introduce themselves and share their learning journey. Their confidence, determination, and enthusiasm deeply moved everyone in the audience, including myself.

Watching them participate alongside other students reminded me that technology should be accessible to everyone. With determination, perseverance, and a supportive community, anyone can learn, develop their skills, and contribute to the technology industry.

This was undoubtedly the most inspiring moment of the entire event.
